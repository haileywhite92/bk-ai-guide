New & Notable

Changes from the past 7 days that actually affect your work — not just AI hype.

📅 Updated June 24, 2026
June 15, 2026 ⚙️ Zapier AI 💰 Cost Impact
AI steps now cost up to 3–5× more by default
Zapier switched to model-tier pricing for AI steps: Standard (1×), Advanced (3× — new default for every AI step), Premium (5×). If you use AI steps in Zaps, your task count may have jumped without you changing anything. Log in and check your usage dashboard. Switch to Standard tier where your logic is simple and doesn't need heavy reasoning.
June 15, 2026 💻 Claude / Anthropic ✅ Good News
Planned billing change paused — Claude usage still covered by your plan
Anthropic paused the Agent SDK billing change before it took effect. The guide had previously noted this as live — it isn't. If you use Claude Code or any automated Claude workflows, your usage still draws from your subscription as before. No new charges.
June 22, 2026 📓 NotebookLM 🆕 New Feature
NotebookLM now remembers between sessions
Memory retention is live — NotebookLM now tracks past interactions so ongoing research projects maintain continuity across sessions. Data is private to your account and can be disabled anytime. Still free. Useful if you're using NotebookLM as a running research hub for a client, launch, or project.
June 1–3, 2026 🏗️ Lovable 🆕 Major Launch
Lovable Cloud: fullstack AI apps with no external services needed
Lovable Cloud now handles database, authentication, file storage, and AI integrations out of the box — no Supabase setup, no API key juggling. You describe the app, it ships the infrastructure. Also launched: Reusable Skills (save your recurring instructions — design systems, SEO rules, tone of voice — and they apply automatically) and a Google Cloud partnership expanding access to Claude + Gemini models inside Lovable.
May 29, 2026 🌐 Perplexity 🆕 New Platform
Perplexity Computer is now built into Microsoft 365
Perplexity's autonomous research tool is now available inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. If your clients or team live in Microsoft 365, they can now run deep multi-source research without switching tabs. No separate Perplexity account required for the basic integration.
June 2026 ⚡ Grok / xAI 🆕 New Tool
Grok for Microsoft Word is free — and covers Excel & PowerPoint too
xAI launched a free Microsoft 365 add-in that brings Grok directly into Word, PowerPoint, and Excel. It drafts content, rewrites for clarity, pulls in live web research and real-time X data, and connects to SharePoint and email. Add it free from the Microsoft Marketplace — no xAI subscription required.

Industry & Platform News

Broader shifts affecting your strategy — beyond the tools in this guide.

June 2026 🔴 Meta Ads 🆕 New Integration
Claude and ChatGPT can now connect directly to your Meta Ads account
Meta opened its ad ecosystem to third-party AI tools via MCP connectors. You can now give Claude or ChatGPT access to your Ads Manager data, pull campaign insights, and draft strategy — without leaving the chat interface. Also: the Meta AI Business Assistant is now in open beta for every advertiser and agency worldwide. Advantage+ campaigns are reporting 22% higher ROAS on average, and 4 million advertisers are now using Meta's generative AI tools.
June 2026 💬 ChatGPT / OpenAI 🆕 New Ad Channel
ChatGPT is now an ad platform — open to any US business, no minimum spend
OpenAI opened self-serve advertising to all US businesses. ChatGPT processes 2.5 billion daily prompts and hit $100M in annualized ad revenue in its first 6 weeks. For coaches and consultants, this is a new top-of-funnel channel worth monitoring — the audience skews toward early AI adopters actively seeking tools and expertise. OpenAI is also building tools to let advertisers generate, localize, and optimize ad creative with AI.
Early 2026 (ongoing) 🔍 Google Search ⚠️ Strategy Impact
Ranking #1 on Google no longer guarantees an AI Overview citation
In mid-2025, 76% of Google AI Overview citations went to top-10 ranked pages. By early 2026, that dropped to 38%. Traditional SEO and AI search visibility have officially decoupled — they now require different strategies. If your content marketing relies on organic Google traffic, this needs attention. Google AI Mode also crossed 1 billion monthly active users, and AI Overviews are at 2.5 billion — search behavior is shifting fast.
Effective August 2, 2026 🇪🇺 EU AI Act ⚠️ Regulatory
EU AI Act enters full enforcement next month — relevant if you serve EU audiences
The EU AI Act's main enforcement phase begins August 2, 2026. Tools like Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini are already required to be transparent about AI-generated content for EU users. Meta is the notable holdout — it refused to sign the GPAI Code of Practice that Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, and OpenAI all signed. No fines have been issued yet. If you have clients or audiences in the EU, this is worth a quick check with your legal or compliance contacts.
June 2026 💼 Microsoft Copilot 💰 Cost Impact
Word, Excel & PowerPoint AI features now require a paid Copilot license
Microsoft tightened its Copilot licensing: AI features inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and OneNote now require a Microsoft 365 Copilot add-on (paid). If your team has been using these features under a standard M365 plan, verify your license status before August — you may hit a paywall. Free Copilot Chat remains available via the web app. On the upside, Copilot now includes GPT-5.5 Instant and Claude Opus 4.7 for those with the paid tier.

Glossary: Key Terms Explained

Plain-English definitions for the jargon you'll encounter.

🤖 LLM
Large Language Model. The AI "brain" behind most chat tools. Examples: GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini. They predict and generate text based on your input.
🎛️ Model
The specific version of an AI. Claude Sonnet vs. Opus, GPT-4o vs. GPT-3.5. Bigger models = more capable but slower and costlier.
📝 Prompt
The instruction or question you type to the AI. Better prompts = better results. Think of it as how you talk to the tool.
🔑 API
Application Programming Interface. A way for developers to connect AI models directly into their own apps or workflows. Requires coding or automation tools.
🪙 Token
How AI measures text. Roughly 1 token = 1 word (or part of a word). API costs are often charged per token used. More tokens = more cost.
📏 Context Window
How much text the AI can "see" and remember at once in one conversation. Claude and Gemini lead here with 100K–1M token windows.
🤝 MCP
Model Context Protocol. A standard that lets AI connect to external tools and services — like your Google Drive, Slack, or ad accounts — so it can read and act on real data.
🕵️ Agent / Agentic AI
An AI that doesn't just answer questions — it takes actions. Agents can browse the web, write files, click buttons, or complete multi-step tasks on your behalf.
🖥️ Computer Use
A capability that lets an AI see and control a computer screen — clicking, typing, navigating — as if it were a human at the keyboard.
🛠️ Skill / System Prompt
Hidden instructions that tell an AI how to behave before you ever type a message. Used to give AI a role, set rules, or load context automatically.
🌡️ Temperature
A setting that controls how "creative" vs. "precise" the AI is. Low temperature = consistent, factual. High temperature = more surprising, varied output.
🔮 Hallucination
When an AI makes up something that sounds plausible but isn't true. All LLMs do this. Always verify important facts, especially with ChatGPT and older models.
🔓 RAG
Retrieval-Augmented Generation. A technique where AI pulls from your own documents or database before answering — reducing hallucinations. NotebookLM uses this.
⚡ Fine-tuning
Training an existing AI model on your own data to make it better at your specific tasks. Requires technical setup. Usually done via API.
🔄 Multimodal
An AI that can work with more than just text — images, audio, video, documents. ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are all multimodal.
